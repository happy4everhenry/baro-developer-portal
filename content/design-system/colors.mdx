<InfoBox type="fun" title="Why red = UP in Korea?">
In Korean (and most East Asian) markets, **red means price went up** and **blue means price went down**. This is the opposite of US and European markets. Think of red envelopes at Lunar New Year -- red is lucky and positive in Korean culture.
</InfoBox>

## Brand Colors

BARO follows the **60-30-10 rule** used by top fintech brands like Stripe, Bloomberg, and N26. The palette has three tiers: neutrals (60%), primary (30%), and accents (10%).

<InfoBox type="info" title="Semantic Rule">
**Indigo** = AI is speaking. **Gold** = "look here" (the signal). **Teal** = data visualization and AI tech. **Red/Blue** = price data ONLY, never as UI accents -- saturated colors trigger impulsive behavior.
</InfoBox>

## Price Semantics <StatusBadge status="live">Korean Convention</StatusBadge>

Korean financial markets use the opposite color convention from Western markets. This is culturally embedded and must be maintained across all financial data.

<SwatchGrid>
  <ColorSwatch name="klPriceUp" hex="#E04848" />
  <ColorSwatch name="klPriceDown" hex="#3478F6" />
</SwatchGrid>

| Token | Hex | Meaning | Used for |
|-------|-----|---------|----------|
| `klPriceUp` | `#E04848` | Price Increase / Gains | Positive returns, upward movement, buy signals |
| `klPriceDown` | `#3478F6` | Price Decrease / Losses | Negative returns, downward movement, sell signals |

<InfoBox type="danger" title="Never swap red and blue">
Korean users (and East Asian markets generally) expect **Red = Up, Blue = Down**. This is the opposite of US/European convention. Incorrect usage will confuse users and violate FSC compliance expectations. Never use green for price increases.
</InfoBox>
