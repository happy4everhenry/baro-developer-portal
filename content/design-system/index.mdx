# Design System

<InfoBox type="fun" title="What's a design system?">
Imagine you have a coloring book, but it comes with rules: "Always use *this* shade of blue for the sky" and "Always leave *this much* space between drawings." A **design system** is exactly that — a set of rules that make sure every part of BARO looks beautiful and consistent, no matter who is building it.
</InfoBox>

A design system is the **single source of truth** for how BARO looks and feels. Every color, font, spacing value, and animation is defined here so the whole app feels like one seamless experience.

## Visual Tokens vs. Motion

<Tabs>
  <Tab label="Visual Tokens" icon="🎨">
    ### The Look of BARO

    Visual tokens are the **building blocks of appearance**. Think of them like a paint palette and a ruler — they define what things look like when they're sitting still.

    <FeatureGrid columns={2}>
      <FeatureCard icon="🎨" title="Colors" description="Every shade in BARO's palette — backgrounds, text, accents, and semantic colors like green for 'up' and red for 'down'" href="/design-system/colors" color="jade" />
      <FeatureCard icon="🔤" title="Typography" description="Font sizes from 11px to 34px, weights from regular to bold, and line heights that make text easy to read" href="/design-system/typography" color="blue" />
      <FeatureCard icon="📏" title="Spacing" description="A scale from 2px to 48px that keeps padding and margins consistent everywhere" href="/design-system/spacing" color="amber" />
      <FeatureCard icon="⬜" title="Corners" description="From sharp rectangles to pill-shaped buttons — 5 levels of roundness" href="/design-system/corners" color="purple" />
    </FeatureGrid>

    <InfoBox type="tip" title="Why tokens?">
    Instead of typing "color: #1A73E8" over and over, developers use a **token** like `klineBlue`. If we ever change the blue, it updates everywhere at once — like magic!
    </InfoBox>
  </Tab>
  <Tab label="Motion & Animation" icon="🎬">
    ### The Feel of BARO

    Motion is what makes BARO feel **alive**. When a chart slides in, a button bounces on tap, or a screen fades away — that's motion design at work.

    <FeatureGrid columns={2}>
      <FeatureCard icon="🎬" title="Motion Curves" description="Spring, easeInOut, and linear — the physics of how things move" href="/design-system/motion" color="red" />
      <FeatureCard icon="🔄" title="Transitions" description="Full-screen animations when navigating between screens" href="/design-system/transitions" color="blue" />
      <FeatureCard icon="👆" title="Micro-interactions" description="Small, satisfying animations — button presses, pull-to-refresh, loading shimmers" href="/design-system/micro-interactions" color="amber" />
      <FeatureCard icon="🌓" title="Theme Switching" description="The smooth crossfade when toggling between light and dark mode" href="/design-system/themes" color="purple" />
    </FeatureGrid>

    <InfoBox type="info" title="Motion has rules too!">
    Every animation in BARO uses standard durations: **150ms** for quick feedback, **300ms** for element transitions, and **500ms** for big screen changes. This keeps the app feeling snappy but smooth.
    </InfoBox>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
