## Brand Story

**The Problem.** Retail investors lose money because they trade on emotion. Most investing apps increase transaction volume, not investor competence. The result: a cycle of FOMO buys, panic sells, and eroded confidence.

**The Insight.** In Korean, there is a concept called **jeongseok (정석)** -- the proven, fundamentally correct approach that stands the test of time. In technical analysis, this is the golden cross, the support level that holds, the volume confirmation that validates a breakout. What if an AI could narrate these proven patterns -- walking you through the correct way to read data, charts, and risk?

**The Name.** "Baro" means "correctly" -- to see correctly, to understand correctly, to act correctly. It embodies the Korean scholarly ideal of the **seonbi (선비)**: rational, disciplined, evidence-driven. BARO is part of the K-culture wave: K-pop, K-beauty, K-drama, and now **K-finance**.

**The Vision.** A generation of Korean investors who see the market correctly. Not dependent on AI, but educated by it. Investors who have learned the proven approach to reading charts, managing risk, and making rational decisions -- because BARO taught them how.

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice Principles

- **Mentor, not salesperson.** Guide decisions like a scholar guides a student -- never push them.
- **Precise financial terminology.** Use proper terms -- moving average, volume profile, support level.
- **Data before opinion.** Always show evidence first, then interpretation.
- **Korean terms naturally alongside English.** Bilingual fluency, not forced translation.
- **Concise declarative statements.** Say what is. No hedging, no hype.

### Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Market neutral | Informative, steady | "Samsung closed at 72,400 won, up 0.8% on moderate volume." |
| Market volatile | Calm, reassuring | "The market is moving sharply. Let me walk you through what's driving this." |
| About to trade | Careful, guiding | "Before confirming, let's review the risk factors together." |
| Educational | Warm, encouraging | "Great observation. This pattern is called a golden cross -- here is why it matters." |
| Achievement | Quietly proud | "Your portfolio is up 12% this quarter. Let's review what worked." |

<InfoBox type="danger" title="Voice Don'ts">
Never use urgency language ("Act now", "Don't miss out"). No gambling metaphors ("bet", "hot tip", "moonshot"). No oversimplification that removes context. No promises of returns. No exclamation marks in UI copy.
</InfoBox>

| Role | Font | Weights | Usage |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| Primary | Inter | 300--900 | All UI text |
| Code/Data | JetBrains Mono | 400--600 | Prices, charts, data |
| Korean | Noto Sans KR | 400--700 | Korean language text |

### Type Scale

| Size | Weight | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| 32px | Light (300) | Display |
| 24px | Regular (400) | Heading |
| 18px | Medium (500) | Subheading |
| 14px | Regular (400) | Body text (default) |
| 12px | Regular (400) | Caption, labels, metadata |

### Typography Principles

- **Hierarchy through weight, not size.** Keep sizes restrained; use weight variation to create contrast.
- **Korean and English in harmony.** When mixing scripts, Noto Sans KR aligns with Inter at the same size.
- **Monospace for numbers.** All financial data uses JetBrains Mono for consistent column alignment.
- **No decorative fonts.** The brand is analytical, not playful. Typography should feel precise and trustworthy.

## Korean Market Conventions

### Price Color Convention

<SwatchGrid>
  <ColorSwatch name="Price Up" hex="#E04848" description="Korean price increase -- Red" />
  <ColorSwatch name="Price Down" hex="#3478F6" description="Korean price decrease -- Blue" />
</SwatchGrid>

<InfoBox type="danger" title="Critical Cultural Rule">
In Korean financial markets, **red indicates price increases** and **blue indicates price decreases** -- the opposite of Western markets. This is culturally embedded and must be maintained across all financial data displays. Never swap these colors.
</InfoBox>

### Korean Language Guidelines

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Financial terms** | Use Korean industry-standard terms (시가총액, 거래량, 이동평균선) |
| **Numbers** | Korean number formatting -- 10,000 won with comma separators |
| **Honorifics** | Use polite formal style (합니다/입니다) in all UI copy |
| **Mixed script** | English for brand name (BARO) and technical terms, Korean for UI labels |
