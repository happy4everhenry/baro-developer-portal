# Glossary

## Your Stock Market Dictionary

Ever heard someone say "the KOSPI is up 2%" or "check the P/E ratio" and thought... *huh?*

You're not alone! The stock market is full of confusing words. This glossary is here to fix that. Every term is explained in **plain, simple language** that anyone can understand — no finance degree required.

<InfoBox type="fun" title="Think of it like a video game guide">
When you play a new video game, there are all kinds of new words — "HP," "mana," "inventory," "XP." At first it's confusing, but once someone explains them, the game makes way more sense! This glossary does the same thing, but for the stock market "game."
</InfoBox>

<InfoBox type="warning" title="Korean Market Convention — Colors Are Flipped!">
In **Korea**, stock market colors work **opposite** to the US:

- **Red means the price is going UP** (good news!)
- **Blue means the price is going DOWN** (not so good)

In America, it's the other way around. So if you see red numbers in BARO, don't panic — it actually means things are going well! This follows the Korean convention used by the Korea Exchange (KRX) and all Korean brokerages.
</InfoBox>

<InfoBox type="note" title="Every term includes Korean">
Since BARO focuses on the Korean stock market, most glossary entries include the **Korean term** alongside the English one. For example, you'll see "Stock (주식)" so you can recognize the word in both languages!
</InfoBox>
